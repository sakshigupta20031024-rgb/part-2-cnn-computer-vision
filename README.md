# part-2-cnn-computer-vision

What is convolution?


Convolution is a process where the CNN scans small parts of an image to detect patterns such as edges, scratches, or stains.

Why is pooling used?


Pooling reduces image size while keeping important features. This makes training faster and helps reduce overfitting.

Why is ReLU commonly used in CNNs?


ReLU helps the model learn faster and improves performance by introducing non-linearity.

Why are CNNs better than regular feed-forward networks for image data?


CNNs are designed specifically for images. They automatically detect visual patterns and require fewer parameters than regular neural networks.
